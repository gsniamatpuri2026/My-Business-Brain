## BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES

**Voice attributes:**

**Direct** — In practice: say what's true, say it plainly, say it early. Do: "This programme isn't right for your profile, and here's why." Don't: "There are several options to consider depending on various factors."

**Substantive** — In practice: every claim is backed by something specific — a real situation, a traceable fact, a concrete outcome. Do: "A rejected application leaves a record. The next consultant has to answer for it." Don't: "We bring years of expertise and a commitment to excellence."

**Warm without softening** — In practice: the care for clients is real and comes through — but it never dilutes the honesty. Do: "This is a significant decision and it deserves a straight answer." Don't: Generic empathy language that could have been written by anyone.

**Grounded** — In practice: rooted in the real world of how things work — how files get read, what case officers actually look for, what documentation genuinely requires. Do: Name the specific mechanism. Don't: Use vague authority signals like "industry-leading" or "comprehensive."

**Unhurried** — In practice: Gurmel's voice doesn't rush to close. It takes the time to give the full picture. Do: Let a point breathe — explain the why, not just the what. Don't: Bullet-list your way through something that needs a sentence.
