## ONE AVATAR: ARJUN

Arjun is fifty-one. He runs operations across India and Hong Kong — importing, distributing, building. He has a flat in Kowloon, a house in Punjab, and a passport that opens some doors and closes others. He doesn't talk about the doors it closes. He just runs the calculation silently at every border, every visa application, every time a deal requires travel somewhere complicated.

He has done well by any measure. But he has also noticed, over the years, that people who look like him work harder to be taken half as seriously in certain rooms. He has absorbed that. He has pushed through it. And he has quietly decided that his children should not have to.

He has known for three or four years that he should do something about the passport situation. He just hasn't found the right person yet. The last one took his money, promised results, and went quiet. That experience cost him more than the fee. It cost him time, and trust, and a failed application that now sits on his record.

**The turning point trigger:**
A political shift in one of his operating markets. A business associate who recently acquired a second citizenship and speaks about it casually, as if it's simply what serious people do. His daughter asking where they would go if things changed.

**Their internal monologue:**
"I've built this with my own hands. I'm not in crisis. But I'm not prepared either — and prepared people don't get caught out the way I've seen others get caught out. I know I should have sorted this years ago. I tried. Someone took my money and showed me what happens when you trust the wrong person. I'm not ready to try again unless I find someone I can actually trust. Someone who knows what they're talking about. Someone who will tell me the truth even if the truth isn't what I want to hear. I've earned enough to make this happen. I just need the right person to make it happen properly."

**Philosophical:** "A man who has spent his whole life building something real shouldn't have to carry the weight of a passport lottery that had nothing to do with his effort or his worth."
