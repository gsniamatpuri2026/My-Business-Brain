Passport ranking means nothing until you ask this one question first. Most RBI and CBI clients come to me after months of research. They've compared Malta, Portugal, the Caribbean. Which passport opens 180 countries versus 168. Then I ask them one question. "What problem are you actually trying to solve?" The silence tells me everything. Because passport rankings are a distraction. It's the detail you focus on before defining what you actually need. A common scenario: a client wants a Caribbean passport for visa-free travel. Clean goal. What they often haven't factored: their US-facing business structure means FATCA complications. The passport works fine. The situation it creates does not. Here's the mistake. They choose the program, then reverse-engineer a reason to want it. The right order: Define the actual outcome first. Tax flexibility? Business continuity? Family security? Second residency without an ongoing tax obligation? Then find the program that fits. Not the other way around. Investment thresholds rise and programs close. Portugal axed its golden visa property route in 2023. The UK ended Tier 1 in 2022. The clients who got stuck were waiting for certainty. The ones who moved had clarity about what they needed. Clarity before commitment. Every time. P.S. If you're comparing programs before you've defined your goal, you're shopping for a solution you haven't written the brief for yet.

A note on timing: most programs take 12 to 24 months from application to grant. The clients who treat CBI as a "just in case" option usually make this decision when urgency is highest and runway is shortest. The time to plan is before you need it.
